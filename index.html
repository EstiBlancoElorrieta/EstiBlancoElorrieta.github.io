A little bit about me:

I am a junior researcher at the Neuroscience of Language lab at New York University in Abu Dhabi. I received my Master's degree on Neuroscience of language from the Basque Center on Cognition, Brain and Language (BCBL) in collaboration with the University of the Basque Country (UPV - EHU), where I conducted my research project in the field of bilingualism, more specifically, looking for an alternative explanation for asymmetries found when bilinguals switch the language they are using to produce an oral utterance (i.e, asymmetries in switch-cost).

I currently work under Liina Pylkkänen's supervision trying to characterize the brain mechanisms responsible for the semantic combinatorics of language. My other research interests include language production, lexical access and the mental lexicon, specifically when these aspects relate to multilingualism, which I try to investigate using imaging methods such as Magnetoencephalography (MEG) as well as behavioral techniques.
