About Me

Esti Blanco-Elorrieta

I am a fifth year graduate student in Psychology at NYU, working in the Neuroscience of Language lab under Liina Pylkkänen's supervision. My work primarily focuses on understanding the neural architecture of the multilingual brain. Taking the most naturalistic approach possible, I conduct MEG studies looking into i) bilingual lexical access ii) the commonalities between bilingual language production and comprehension, and iii) the adaptive nature of language control mechanisms in different conversational contexts. The goal of my research is to develop a cohesive framework of bilingual language organization, and theories that represent bilingual language use both in, but crucially also out, of experimental settings.

In the Fall of 2020, I will be joining the Cognitive Neuropsychology lab at Harvard University to work under Alfonso Caramazza's supervision.
